Hackathon

24 hours. One problem. Infinite possibilities. Build, break, and deploy something that matters under pressure.

Overview

Teams of 2–4 will receive a problem statement at kick-off and have 24 hours to design and present a working solution. All domains welcome — web, mobile, AI/ML, IoT, and hardware hacks.

Eligibility

  • Open to all undergraduate and postgraduate students
  • Inter-college teams allowed
  • Maximum 4 members per team
  • Each member must register individually

Rules

  • All code must be written during the hackathon window — no pre-built projects
  • Use of open-source libraries and APIs is permitted
  • Teams must present a working demo at the end
  • Judging: Innovation (30%), Technical Complexity (25%), Presentation (25%), Feasibility (20%)
  • Organiser decisions are final

Prizes

🏉 1st Place₹25,000
🥈 2nd Place₹15,000
🥉 3rd Place₹10,000
Best Domain Hack₹5,000

Timeline

Oct 10, 14:00Problem statement release & kick-off
Oct 11, 08:00Mid-point mentoring sessions
Oct 12, 09:00Final submission & demo presentations
Oct 12, 17:30Winners announced at valedictory

What to Bring

Laptops, chargers, any hardware components you plan to use. Meals and refreshments will be provided throughout the 24-hour window. On-campus accommodation available for outstation participants.

Capture the Flag

A jeopardy-style cybersecurity competition. Find the flags. Exploit the systems. Own the scoreboard.

Overview

Participants solve challenges across multiple cybersecurity domains to retrieve hidden "flags". Challenges increase in difficulty and point value. Top score wins.

Domains Covered

  • Web Exploitation
  • Reverse Engineering
  • Forensics & Steganography
  • Cryptography
  • Binary Exploitation (PWN)
  • OSINT

Rules

  • Teams of 1–3 participants
  • No attacking other teams' infrastructure
  • Flag sharing or trading is strictly prohibited
  • Automated scanners against the platform are banned
  • All actions are logged — violations result in disqualification

Prizes

🏉 1st Place₹15,000
🥈 2nd Place₹10,000
🥉 3rd Place₹5,000

Timeline

Oct 11, 10:00Platform opens, competition begins
Oct 11, 18:30Scoreboard freeze
Oct 11, 19:00Competition closes, results declared

Robowar

Build a combat robot and destroy the competition. The arena is unforgiving. Only the strongest machine survives.

Overview

Teams bring pre-built combat robots to a custom arena. Robots battle in a knockout format. Points also awarded for aggression and control.

Bot Specifications

  • Weight: Max 15 kg
  • Dimensions: Max 60×60×30 cm
  • Power: 24V DC max
  • No flammable fluids or lasers
  • Remote control (2.4GHz) required
  • All bots inspected before competition

Rules

  • Teams of 2–5 members
  • Bots must be brought by Day 1 for technical inspection
  • Matches are 3 minutes; if no KO, judges decide on points
  • Repairs allowed between rounds (30 min window)
  • Any weapon causing arena damage will be disqualified

Prizes

🏉 1st Place₹20,000
🥈 2nd Place₹12,000
🥉 3rd Place₹8,000
Best Design₹3,000

Timeline

Oct 10, 15:30Technical inspection & registration
Oct 10, 16:30Preliminary rounds begin
Oct 12, 12:00Semi-finals & Finals

Code Sprint

Competitive programming stripped to its core. Solve algorithmic problems fast. Think faster.

Overview

A timed competitive programming contest featuring 8–12 problems of increasing difficulty. Ranked by problems solved, then penalty time.

Allowed Languages

  • C / C++ (GCC)
  • Python 3
  • Java
  • No internet access permitted
  • Offline documentation only

Format & Rules

  • Round 1 (Day 1): 3 hours, 8 problems — top 20 advance
  • Finals (Day 2): 2.5 hours, 6 harder problems
  • Solo or pairs (pairs share one machine)
  • Wrong submissions carry +20 min penalty
  • Internet and external communication strictly prohibited

Prizes

🏉 1st Place₹12,000
🥈 2nd Place₹8,000
🥉 3rd Place₹5,000

Timeline

Oct 10, 12:00Round 1 begins
Oct 10, 15:00Round 1 ends, qualifiers announced
Oct 11, 14:00Finals begin
Oct 11, 16:30Results & winners

Paper Presentation

Your research, your ideas, your voice. Present original or review work to a panel of industry and academic judges.

Overview

Participants present a technical paper in a 10-minute presentation followed by a 5-minute Q&A. Papers must be submitted in advance for review.

Tracks

  • Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
  • IoT & Embedded Systems
  • Cybersecurity & Networking
  • Sustainable & Green Tech
  • Open Track (any CS/ECE topic)

Submission Guidelines

  • Paper length: 4–8 pages (IEEE format preferred)
  • Submit abstract by September 20, full paper by September 30
  • Solo or paired authorship only
  • Plagiarism check — max 20% similarity
  • Accepted papers featured in Resonance '25 proceedings

Prizes

🏉 Best Paper₹10,000
🥈 Runner Up₹6,000
Best Presentation₹4,000

Timeline

Sep 20Abstract submission deadline
Sep 30Full paper deadline
Oct 11, 10:30Presentations — Session A

Circuit Blitz

You'll get a problem. You'll get components. You'll get two hours. Make it work.

Overview

Teams receive a problem statement and a bag of electronic components. They must design, simulate, and physically build a working circuit solution within the time limit.

Provided Components

  • Basic passives (R, C, L)
  • Op-amps, comparators
  • Arduino Uno / Nano
  • Breadboards & jumper wires
  • Sensors (surprise, team-specific)
  • Oscilloscope & multimeter access

Rules

  • Teams of 2–3 members
  • Problem statement revealed at start — no advance prep
  • Only provided components may be used
  • No internet access during build phase
  • Circuits tested by judges on a standard bench
  • Bonus points for power efficiency and clean design

Prizes

🏉 1st Place₹10,000
🥈 2nd Place₹6,000
🥉 3rd Place₹4,000

Timeline

Oct 12, 10:00Problem reveal & build begins
Oct 12, 12:30Build phase ends
Oct 12, 14:30Demo & judging
Oct 12, 17:30Results at valedictory
